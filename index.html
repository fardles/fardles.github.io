Shelford Hill

And what is earth's eye, tongue or heart else, where
Else, but in dear and dogged man?

Gerard Manley Hopkins, "Ribblesdale" (1918)

This is where he lives, on the top of the hill that is no longer a hill. Contours visible and invisible, once felt, now barely felt, elevator land and only imposing when, trying to push a pram up the side, sweat beads form, jostle and sway.
